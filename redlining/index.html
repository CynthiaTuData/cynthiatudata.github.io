Redlining and Highway Building in NYC

This map documents HOLC grades given to neighborhoods in New York City in the 1930s, layered with the current-day highway system in New York City to show how and where the city's urban planners planned for the highways.
Highways tend to cut through neighborhoods that were graded "C" or "D," which usually means poorer or highly Black/immigrant populated communities.

HOLC Grades

Grade A: Best
Grade B: Desirable
Grade C: Declining
Grade D: Hazardous
